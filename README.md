# 🌿 Butter & Bloom — Visual Design Summary

## **Brand Aesthetic**
Butter & Bloom uses a **warm botanical patisserie aesthetic**, combining soft natural tones with premium dessert styling. The brand aims for an **artisanal, elegant, and inviting** feel that reflects handcrafted quality.

---

## **Colour Palette**
- **Cream (#FDF8F2)** — primary background tone for warmth and softness.  
- **Botanical Green (#6B7D6A)** — grounding accent used for headings, borders, and subtle UI elements.  
- **Golden Brown (#C7A27A)** — premium highlight colour used for CTAs, decorative accents, and hero text.  
- **Cherry / Raspberry Red (#C94A4A)** — vibrant accent used sparingly for emphasis (Order/Subscribe buttons).  
- **Reduced‑saturation dessert tones** — muted pinks, sage greens, taupes, and raspberry shades used in photography and section backgrounds.

These colours create a **cohesive, organic, and premium dessert atmosphere**.

---

## **Typography**
- **Great Vibes (Script)** — used in the logo for a handcrafted, elegant patisserie feel.  
- **Serif Subtext** — reinforces premium craftsmanship in the logo.  
- **Sans‑serif body text** — clean, modern, and readable for UI and content sections.

Typography balances **romantic patisserie charm** with **modern clarity**.

---

## **Logo Style**
- Gold script lettering  
- Serif subtext  
- Floral cake illustration  
- Symmetrical decorative accents  

The logo communicates **handcrafted quality**, **botanical freshness**, and **premium dessert artistry**.


## **Imagery Style**
Butter & Bloom’s imagery focuses on **warm, artisanal dessert presentation** with a cohesive botanical theme.

- **Multiple desserts in one hero image** to showcase richness, variety, and abundance.  
- **Soft botanical styling** using flowers, leaves, and natural textures to reinforce the brand’s floral identity.  
- **Reduced saturation** across all photos to maintain colour harmony with the brand palette.  
- **Seasonal dessert photography** such as lemon elderflower cake, raspberry swirl cheesecake, and floral shortbread cookies.  
- **Clean, neutral backgrounds** to keep attention on product detail and craftsmanship.

Images are curated to feel **fresh, artisanal, and colour‑cohesive**, supporting the brand’s warm premium aesthetic.

**Site Owner’s Primary Goal (Business):**
- To showcase baked goods and encourage customers to contact the bakery for custom orders and visits.

- What the bakery wants from the website:

- Encourage custom order inquiries

- Attract new customers

**External User’s Primary Goal (Customer):**
- To easily browse the bakery’s products and submit a custom order or inquiry for baked goods. 

**External User Goals (Visitors / Customers)**
- What a user wants when they visit the bakery site: 
- View available baked goods  
- See prices or product descriptions  
- Decide if the bakery fits their taste/style  
- Request a custom order (cake, pastries)  
- Find bakery location and opening hours  
- Contact the bakery easily

### Design choices
**Research: Competitor Review**

Competitor Pros — Features Other Bakery Sites Do Well:
- Subscription pop‑ups — Many bakeries offer 10–15% off for first‑time customers to build an email list and encourage repeat orders.
- Seasonal product highlights — Valentine’s, Easter, Christmas, or summer menus with themed visuals boost urgency and sales.
- High‑quality hero photography — Clean, bright, close‑up shots immediately communicate quality and price point.
- Clear product categorisation — “Celebration Cakes”, “Cupcakes”, “Wedding”, “Corporate”, “Dietary Options”.
- Story/brand section — Short founder stories build trust and emotional connection.
- Social proof — Testimonials, Google reviews, Instagram embeds, customer photos.
- Delivery & collection info — Clear “How it works” sections explaining lead times, delivery zones, pricing.
- Interactive flavour menus — Icons, colour coding, or hover effects.
- Custom order forms — Dropdowns, checkboxes, image uploads reduce back‑and‑forth messaging.
- Instagram‑style gallery — Grid of past bakes builds credibility.
- Allergen transparency — Icons or dedicated pages build trust.

Competitor Cons — Common Weaknesses You Can Improve On:
- Cluttered layouts — Too many images, colours, fonts.
- Inconsistent branding — Mismatched colours and uneven spacing.
- Poor mobile optimisation — Overlapping text, cropped images, unusable forms.
- Slow loading times — Huge image files slow mobile browsing.
- No clear pricing — Hidden prices frustrate users.
- Weak product descriptions — Missing flavour notes, portion sizes, serving suggestions.
- Confusing navigation — Too many menu items or unclear labels.
- No upsells — No add‑ons like candles, toppers, matching cupcakes.
- Outdated design trends — Old‑fashioned shadows, gradients.
- No accessibility considerations — Low contrast text, missing alt tags.
- No subscription strategy — Many don’t use email marketing at all.

**Requirements**

User Need Classification  
*(What users say they need vs. what they actually need vs. what they don’t realize they need)*

---

What Users Say They Need  
Explicit, conscious requests users expect from a bakery website.

- Product / menu page with categories 
- Business hours  
- Contact information
- **Image gallery of baked goods 
- Custom order request form  
- Allergen information

---

What Users Actually Need  
Requirements necessary for usability, even if not verbalized.

- Clear navigation structure
- Mobile-first responsive layout  
- Logical content hierarchy  
- Readable typography and spacing 
- Simple, short forms  
- Clear calls-to-action (Order, Contact, Subscribe)

---

What Users Didn’t Know They Needed  
Unexpected features that improve trust and engagement.
- Seasonal highlights section  
- Email subscription with discount incentive  
- Upsell add-ons (candles, toppers)  
- Featured items on home page 
- Trust-building “About Us” section

---

**Requirement Types**

---

**Objective Requirements**  
What the user wants to accomplish.
- Browse bakery products  
- Decide what to order  
- Request a custom cake  
- Check hours and location  
- Ensure food safety (allergens)  
- Get discounts or updates  
- Contact the bakery  

---

**Functional Requirements**
What the user must be able to do.
- Navigate between Home, Menu, Gallery, and Order sections  
- View categorized product listings  
- View product images  
- Submit a custom order request form  
- Submit a booking or inquiry form  
- Subscribe via email form  
- Read allergen labels  
- View seasonal offerings  
- View upsell add-ons as selectable options  

> Even if forms are non-functional, they still count because the interaction exists.

---

**Non‑Functional Requirements** 
Constraints the site must operate within.
- Static HTML and CSS only (no backend)  
- Mobile-first responsive design  
- Fast load time  
- Cross-browser compatibility  
- Accessible color contrast and font sizes  
- Forms do not store or submit real data  
- Content is informational and demonstrative
